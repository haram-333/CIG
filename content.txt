Welcome to College for Innovation and Growth
College for Innovation and Growth is a UK-based higher education provider committed to delivering accessible, inclusive, and career-focused undergraduate study in Bristol.
Our aim is to create meaningful pathways into higher education for learners from diverse, disadvantaged, and underrepresented backgrounds. We support students who may not have followed a traditional academic route, including mature learners, adult learners, early school leavers, unemployed adults, underemployed individuals, and those without standard qualifications.
We believe higher education should open doors, raise aspirations, and support long-term career progression. Our proposed programmes are designed to help students develop academic knowledge, professional confidence, practical skills, and employability readiness.
Higher Education That Supports Progression
At College for Innovation and Growth, we focus on delivering education that is relevant to students, employers, and regional workforce needs.
Our proposed higher education provision is designed around:
•	Widening participation and fair access
•	Foundation Year pathways into undergraduate study
•	Flexible routes for mature and non-traditional learners
•	Career-focused teaching and learning
•	Strong academic and pastoral support
•	Employability and progression planning

Proposed Undergraduate Programmes

BA (Hons) Business and Tourism Management with Foundation Year
Course Overview
The BA (Hons) Business and Tourism Management with Foundation Year is designed for students who want to develop knowledge and skills in business, tourism, hospitality, management, customer experience, enterprise, and service industries.
The programme aims to prepare students for opportunities in business operations, tourism, hospitality, events, marketing, customer service, and management-related sectors.
The Foundation Year provides an accessible route into undergraduate study for students who may not currently meet standard entry requirements for direct degree entry or who would benefit from additional academic preparation.
Why Study This Course?
Bristol continues to experience growth across business, tourism, hospitality, and professional services. Employers increasingly seek graduates with strong communication, leadership, customer service, commercial awareness, and management skills.
This course is suitable for students who are interested in:
•	Business and organisational management
•	Tourism and hospitality
•	Events and travel industries
•	Customer experience management
•	Marketing and service operations
•	Enterprise and entrepreneurship
•	Leadership and communication
What You May Study
Students may explore areas such as:
•	Introduction to business and management
•	Tourism and hospitality operations
•	Customer experience and service excellence
•	Marketing and communication
•	Business operations and administration
•	Enterprise and entrepreneurship
•	Leadership and teamwork
•	Digital and commercial awareness
•	Academic and professional skills
•	Employability and career planning
Career Opportunities
Graduates may progress into areas such as:
•	Business operations
•	Tourism and hospitality management
•	Events and travel industries
•	Marketing and customer experience
•	Service sector management
•	Enterprise and business development
•	Administration and management roles
•	Further study or professional development
Entry Requirements
Applicants will normally be expected to demonstrate the ability, motivation, and commitment to study at undergraduate level.
Typical entry requirements may include one or more of the following:
•	Level 3 qualification or equivalent
•	Relevant work experience in business, tourism, hospitality, customer service, administration, or related sectors
•	Mature learner profile with appropriate life or professional experience
•	Successful interview or academic assessment, where required
•	Evidence of motivation and commitment to study
•	English language competency for applicants whose first language is not English
Applicants who do not hold traditional qualifications may still be considered through a flexible admissions process, particularly where they can demonstrate relevant experience, potential, and commitment.
Final entry requirements will be confirmed in line with the University of Wolverhampton’s admissions criteria and programme approval requirements.

BSc (Hons) Public Health with Foundation Year
Course Overview
The BSc (Hons) Public Health with Foundation Year is designed for students who want to develop knowledge and skills in public health, health promotion, community wellbeing, preventative healthcare, health inequalities, and public service improvement.
The programme aims to support students who are interested in careers within public health, community health, wellbeing services, social care-related sectors, and NHS-related support environments.
The Foundation Year provides an accessible route into undergraduate study for students who may not currently meet standard entry requirements for direct degree entry or who would benefit from additional academic preparation before progressing onto degree-level learning.
Why Study This Course?
Public health plays an essential role in improving lives, reducing inequalities, and supporting healthier communities.
This course is suitable for students who are interested in:
•	Public health and wellbeing
•	Health promotion
•	Community health support
•	Mental health awareness
•	Preventative healthcare
•	Health inequalities and inclusion
•	Supporting individuals, families, and communities
The programme is designed to help students develop subject knowledge, academic confidence, communication skills, professional awareness, and employability skills relevant to health and community-focused sectors.
What You May Study

Applicants will normally be expected to demonstrate the ability, motivation, and commitment to study at undergraduate level.

Admissions
College for Innovation and Growth is committed to providing a fair, transparent, and accessible admissions process for students who wish to enter higher education.
We understand that every learner’s journey is different. Some applicants may have recent qualifications, while others may be returning to education after time away from formal study, employment, or other responsibilities.
Our admissions approach is designed to recognise academic qualifications, relevant experience, motivation, potential, and readiness for higher education.
Who Can Apply?
We welcome applications from students who are:
•	Aged 18 or above
•	Mature learners returning to education
•	Adults aged 19+ seeking career progression
•	Students with Level 3 qualifications or equivalent
•	Applicants with relevant work or voluntary experience
•	Applicants seeking a Foundation Year route into undergraduate study
•	Learners from disadvantaged or underrepresented backgrounds
•	Learners from non-traditional education backgrounds
General Entry Requirements
Applicants may be considered based on:
•	Academic qualifications
•	Relevant work or voluntary experience
•	Personal statement
•	Interview, where required
•	Academic assessment, where required
•	Motivation and suitability for the course
•	English language ability, where applicable
Flexible Entry Routes
We recognise that not all applicants follow a traditional route into higher education.
Applicants who do not hold standard qualifications may still be considered if they can demonstrate:
•	Relevant professional, voluntary, or life experience
•	Commitment to study
•	Career goals linked to the chosen programme
•	Ability to engage with academic learning
•	Potential to succeed with appropriate support
English Language Requirements
Applicants whose first language is not English may be required to provide evidence of English language ability.
This may include a recognised English language qualification or another form of assessment accepted under the admissions process.
How to Apply
To apply or register your interest, please contact our admissions team.
Applicants may be asked to provide:
•	Completed application form
•	Copies of qualifications
•	Identification documents
•	Personal statement
•	Evidence of work or voluntary experience, where relevant
•	English language evidence, where required
Our admissions team will guide applicants through each stage of the process.
